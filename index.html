Portfolio Enhancement for a New Market Regime

First Principle Asset Management designs structured-note portfolios that convert market volatility into stable income and improved risk-adjusted returns. We help advisors and institutions replace a portion of traditional equity and bond exposure with engineered, downside-aware payoff profiles.

How the Strategy Works See Portfolio Impact

Why Portfolios Need a New Approach

Forward-looking equity returns sit near decade lows, while volatility and macro uncertainty remain elevated. Traditional 60/40 portfolios face compressed return expectations, higher cross-asset correlation, and less reliable diversification.

Lower Forward Returns

Sell-side capital market assumptions from JPMorgan, BlackRock, and Goldman Sachs project the weakest expected equity and 60/40 returns in more than a decade.

Persistent Volatility

Market volatility remains structurally elevated, driven by macro uncertainty, factor dispersion, and geopolitical instability.

Crowded Income Sources

Income sources such as IG and HY credit are increasingly crowded and offer limited incremental yield, while correlations to equities continue to rise.

Our Solution: Turning Volatility Into Income

We design diversified portfolios of structured notes engineered to complement traditional equity and credit exposure. Returns are driven by volatility pricing and outcome-defined payoff structures—not by market timing or directional forecasts.

Volatility as a Source of Income

Our portfolios convert market volatility into consistent income by harvesting option premiums across diversified underlying indices. Payoff mechanics, not market direction, drive returns.

Higher Income with Defined Risk Parameters

Structured-note portfolios deliver elevated cash yields relative to IG and HY credit, without duration risk. Transparent barrier levels tied to broad indices provide clarity on potential outcomes.

Outcome-Driven Behavior Across Market Regimes

Structured-note payoffs behave differently from long-only assets, generating income in range-bound or moderately rising markets and adding asymmetric exposure that complements traditional portfolios.
