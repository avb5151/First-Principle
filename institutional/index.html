The Regime Shift

Capital market assumptions have shifted materially. Forward equity returns are lower, volatility is structurally higher, and traditional diversification is less reliable. The portfolio construction playbook that worked in the QE era requires recalibration for today’s and tomorrow’s regime.

2010–2019: QE-Dominated Regime

Forward Equity Returns 7–9%
Realized Volatility (SPX) 12–15%
60/40 Expected Return 6–7%
Equity–Bond Correlation Negative to Low

Beta and diversification did the heavy lifting; traditional 60/40 portfolios were highly effective.

2020–2025: Elevated Volatility Regime

Forward Equity Returns 4–5%
Realized Volatility (SPX) 18–22%
60/40 Expected Return 4–5%
Equity–Bond Correlation Higher, Less Reliable

Drawdowns became sharper and diversification less dependable; portfolios need more efficient payoff design.

Our Solution: Payoff Engineering

We construct diversified portfolios of structured notes that harvest index option premium across major indices (SPX, NDX, RUT, SX5E). Our approach converts volatility and skew into income and convex payoff profiles, designed to integrate into existing policy portfolios rather than replace them.

Volatility Premium Harvesting

We systematically sell index options across diversified underlyings, converting realized and implied volatility into consistent income streams. Payoff structures are designed to generate returns in range-bound and moderately rising markets, independent of directional forecasts.

Barrier and Coupon Mechanics

Structured notes employ transparent barrier levels (typically 50–80% of initial index level) and coupon structures that define downside participation. This creates outcome-defined payoffs that differ materially from long-only equity or credit exposures.

Portfolio Integration, Not Replacement

Our structured-note sleeves are designed to replace a portion of traditional equity or credit exposure within existing policy portfolios. They are sized and slotted into allocation frameworks, not layered on top, maintaining overall risk budgets while improving income and drawdown characteristics.

Implementation Use Cases

Structured-note portfolios can be integrated into institutional allocations in multiple ways, depending on income targets, drawdown tolerance, and existing policy design. Choose how you want to use the sleeve; we engineer the payoff mechanics and risk management around that role.

Partial Bond Sleeve Replacement

Replace 10–20% of traditional fixed income with structured income sleeves, targeting higher cash yields with defined barrier levels and no duration risk. The sleeve is sized to remain within existing fixed income risk budgets while improving income and drawdown characteristics.

Low-Vol Equity Carve-Out

Reallocate 15–25% of core equity exposure into structured equity sleeves, maintaining equity-like return potential while introducing buffers, path-dependent income, and improved downside asymmetry. The objective is to reshape the distribution of equity outcomes, not simply add leverage.

Alternative Credit Replacement

Substitute structured income portfolios for portions of high-yield or alternative credit allocations, targeting comparable or higher yields with more transparent payoff mechanics and explicit barrier-based risk parameters. This can simplify risk oversight while preserving income.
