A Research-Driven Framework for Structured Products

First Principle Asset Management converts complex option payoffs into systematic, downside-aware portfolio sleeves. Our strategies are designed to enhance income, shape drawdowns, and improve risk-adjusted returns when paired with traditional equity and fixed income. We employ rigorous analytics, scenario testing, and institutional-grade portfolio construction to deliver structured-note portfolios that integrate seamlessly into existing policy allocations.

Instruments & Payoff Mechanics

Our portfolios are constructed from diversified baskets of structured notes on major equity indices. Understanding the instruments and their payoff mechanics is fundamental to evaluating how these sleeves behave across market regimes.

Underlying Indices

We primarily use broad-based equity indices as underlyings: S&P 500 (SPX), NASDAQ-100 (NDX), Russell 2000 (RUT), and Euro Stoxx 50 (SX5E). These indices provide liquidity, transparency, and diversification. We also employ worst-of baskets across multiple indices to enhance yield while maintaining diversification.

Barrier Levels & Downside Protection

Structured notes employ barrier levels that define downside participation. For example, a note with a 70% barrier means that if the underlying index falls below 70% of its initial level at maturity, the investor participates in the loss from that barrier level. Barrier levels typically range from 50% to 80%, depending on the strategy's risk budget and income objectives. Higher barriers provide more protection but generally result in lower coupon rates.

Coupon Structures

Notes pay periodic coupons (typically quarterly or semi-annual) that are funded by the option premium embedded in the structure. Coupon rates vary based on volatility assumptions, barrier levels, and tenor. In range-bound or moderately rising markets, coupons drive most of the return. Coupons may be conditional (only paid if the index remains above a certain level) or unconditional, depending on the structure type.

Autocall Features

Many structures include autocall features that allow early redemption if the underlying index reaches a predefined level (typically 100–110% of initial) on specified observation dates. Autocalls can accelerate returns in favorable markets and reduce time to maturity, improving portfolio turnover and reinvestment opportunities.

Risk & Return Drivers

Understanding the drivers of risk and return in structured-note portfolios is essential for portfolio construction and risk management. Returns are primarily driven by volatility pricing, not directional market forecasts.

Theta Decay / Carry from Sold Options

Structured notes effectively sell options (puts, calls, or combinations) to generate income. The option premium received funds coupon payments. As time passes, all else equal, the value of sold options decays (theta), which is captured as income. This creates a positive carry component that is independent of market direction, as long as the underlying remains within certain bounds.

Volatility & Correlation Regimes

Option values are sensitive to volatility. Higher implied volatility increases option premiums, which can enhance coupon rates. However, realized volatility that exceeds assumptions can lead to more frequent barrier breaches. We model volatility regimes (low vol QE era vs. elevated vol post-2020) and adjust portfolio construction accordingly. Correlation across underlyings also affects basket structures—lower correlation can enhance diversification benefits but may reduce worst-of basket yields.

Tail-Risk Scenarios & Management

In severe market stress (e.g., 2008, 2020), structured notes can experience losses beyond barrier levels. We manage tail risk through: (1) diversification across issuers and underlyings, (2) conservative barrier levels for core strategies, (3) scenario stress testing, and (4) ongoing monitoring of barrier proximity. While structured notes provide downside protection relative to long-only equity, they are not risk-free and can experience material losses in extreme scenarios.

Portfolio Construction Process

Our portfolios are constructed as systematic sleeves, not ad hoc trades. We follow a repeatable process that links client objectives to payoff design, issuer selection, and ongoing risk management.

Laddering Maturities

  • Stagger maturities across 1–5 year tenors
  • Ensures continuous coupon income
  • Reduces reinvestment risk
  • Allows for regime-aware roll management

Diversifying Across Underlyings

  • Allocate across SPX, NDX, RUT, SX5E
  • Limit concentration in any single index
  • Use worst-of baskets for yield enhancement
  • Monitor correlation structure

Managing Issuer Risk

  • Focus on investment-grade issuers
  • Set concentration limits per issuer
  • Ongoing credit monitoring
  • Diversify across multiple financial institutions

Risk Budget Adherence

  • Size sleeves to fit within overall portfolio risk budget
  • Maintain target volatility and drawdown limits
  • Regular rebalancing and barrier monitoring
  • Scenario testing against policy benchmarks
