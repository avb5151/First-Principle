Structured Products 101

Structured products are hybrid investment instruments that combine fixed income securities with derivatives to create customizable payoff profiles. These instruments are designed to provide downside protection, yield enhancement, or targeted exposures to underlying assets while maintaining institutional-grade risk management. First Principle implements structured products through SMA-based platforms, enabling advisors to access these sophisticated strategies within a policy-driven, scalable framework.

Key Building Blocks

Underlying Assets

The reference assets that determine the structured product's performance, which may include equities, indices, commodities, or fixed income instruments. The selection of underlying assets defines the risk-return profile and exposure characteristics.

Protection / Barriers

Downside protection mechanisms that limit capital exposure through barrier levels or principal protection features. These structures are engineered to provide controlled risk parameters while maintaining participation in upside performance.

Coupons / Income

Periodic income payments or coupon structures that provide yield enhancement relative to traditional fixed income. These payments are typically linked to the performance of underlying assets and may be conditional on barrier levels.

Maturity & Liquidity

The term structure and liquidity provisions that define the investment horizon and exit options. First Principle's SMA platform provides continuous access and policy-driven implementation, eliminating calendar-based issuance constraints.

Use Cases for Advisors

Capital Preservation

Structured products can be designed to protect principal while providing participation in market upside, making them suitable for clients seeking downside protection with growth potential.

Yield Enhancement

Through structured coupon payments and income generation, these instruments can provide enhanced yield relative to traditional fixed income, particularly in low-rate environments.

Volatility Management

Structured products offer systematic approaches to managing portfolio volatility through defined risk parameters and engineered exposure profiles that align with client risk tolerance.

Portfolio Diversification

By providing exposure to alternative risk-return profiles and non-correlated payoffs, structured products can enhance portfolio diversification and risk-adjusted returns.
